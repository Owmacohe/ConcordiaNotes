Public happenstance

Late in muddy afternoon

Sifting purposeless along half-used side streets
I chanced on a park that I had not intended to visit

As a humid, yet not overbearing sun

Exposed bare concrete and warmed parked cars
... I stopped

Being in the habit of finding nearby parks

The decision was made to ride in, on bike
To sit, and watch, and write

Now I'm not here to say that Parc Saint-Pierre-Claver

Is a shining beacon of public space ideals
But it's pretty. And it's tiny. And it's green

With a faded industrial backdrop

Falling leaf-sheaths of yellow
And a decent waterpark, it's nice

...

Really nice :)
I hope I can get back soon

-Owen Hellum, 2020
